Biography

Bernard Ghanem is a professor at KAUST, where he leads the Image and Video Understanding Lab (IVUL) and the Center of Excellence in Generative AI. He is also Deputy Director of the KAUST AI Initiative. His research focuses on computer vision, machine learning, large-scale video and 3D understanding, and foundational aspects of machine learning. He previously served as a graduate research assistant at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign under Prof. Narendra Ahuja, working on dynamic textures and activity recognition. He has also been a summer intern at the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University.

Research Interests

Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Large-Scale Video Understanding, 3D Understanding, Foundations of Machine Learning, Pattern Recognition, Digital Image Processing, Video Processing, Object Tracking, Activity Recognition and Detection, Action Recognition, Semantic Segmentation, Deep Neural Networks

Awards and Distinctions

  • Promoted to Full Professor at KAUST, 2022

  • Deputy Director of the AI Initiative at KAUST, 2021–Present

  • Shoman Arab Researcher Award in Machine Learning and Big Data, 2020

  • Invited Contributor to AI Index Report (ActivityNet), 2019

  • Best Paper Award, International Workshop on Computer Vision for UAVs (CVPRW), 2019

  • Ranked First in the NEOM AI Challenge (Entertainment Track), 2020

  • Organizer, ActivityNet Challenge at CVPR (2016–2019)
